About Legal Notices

A legal notice is a formal written communication sent by an advocate on behalf of a client to an opposing party. It outlines the client's grievances, states the legal rights violated, raises a specific demand (e.g. refund, settlement, performance), and warns of imminent court proceedings if the demands are not met.

Why send a legal notice? Sending a notice on an advocate's letterhead demonstrates that you are serious about taking legal action. It gives the recipient a final chance to resolve the matter out of court, saving both parties time and money. It also establishes a clear timeline and forms part of the court evidence if litigation becomes necessary.

Replying to a Notice: If you receive a legal notice, it is crucial to reply within the specified timeline (usually 15 days). Failing to reply can be construed as admitting the facts in court. We draft professional replies defending your legal rights.

2

Eligibility / Who Can Send

Any individual or organization seeking to enforce their legal rights, resolve a grievance, or respond to a threat can send or reply to a legal notice. This includes:

  • Creditors seeking loan recovery
  • Buyers cheated by e-commerce sites, retailers, or builders
  • Employees facing salary withholding or wrongful discharge
  • Landlords demanding eviction or rent clearance
  • Individuals who have received a notice and need to send a legal counter-statement

Required Documents

  • Detailed description of the event / transaction
  • Name, address, and email of the opposite party (recipient)
  • Supporting transactions or invoice documents
  • Prior email communications or chat transcripts
  • The copy of the legal notice received (if you need a reply drafted)
  • Your Identity Proof (Aadhaar Card / PAN Card)
