About Money Recovery Cases

Money recovery cases are legal actions filed to regain outstanding dues from defaulting individuals, customers, or business partners. While standard civil suits can take time, the Code of Civil Procedure (CPC) provides a special fast-track mechanism known as a Summary Suit (under Order 37) for recovering liquidated debts.

Order 37 Summary Suit: This is a powerful tool where the defendant does not have an automatic right to defend the suit. They must apply to the court for "leave to defend" within 10 days of summons, which is granted only if they show a genuine defense. If denied, the court immediately decrees the suit in your favor.

Common defaults: Unpaid friendly loans, unpaid business invoices, withheld rental security deposits, salary dues, or advance payments for services that were never delivered.

2

Eligibility / Who Can File

You can file a money recovery suit if you hold written proof of debt. This includes:

  • Written Loan Agreements or Promissory Notes (Pran-patra)
  • Accepted commercial bills or tax invoices
  • Bank records showing money transfer + WhatsApp/Email acknowledgment of debt
  • Dishonoured cheques or written settlement agreements

Limitation Period: The suit must be filed within 3 years from the date of the loan default or invoice due date. A written acknowledgment of debt within the 3 years extends the limitation by another 3 years.

3

Required Documents

  • Written Loan Agreement / Promissory Note / Acknowledgement Receipt
  • Unpaid invoices / Bills / Delivery receipts
  • Bank Statement showing money transfer to debtor
  • Ledger Account statement of the debtor
  • WhatsApp chats, text messages, or emails requesting repayment
  • Copy of Legal Notice sent to debtor (with postal proof)
  • Government ID of the creditor

Step-by-Step Process

Legal Review & Audit

We review your transaction proof and communication logs to confirm the debt and limitation period.

Send Demand Legal Notice

We send a final 15-day Legal Notice to the debtor, warning them of civil/criminal action.

Filing the Suit (Order 37)

If they refuse to pay, we file a Summary Suit before the Civil Court, attaching all documentary evidence.

Summons of Judgment

The court issues summons. The debtor must enter an appearance within 10 days, otherwise we seek immediate judgment.

Defense Challenge & Trial

Our advocate challenges the debtor's defense application. If granted, we conduct a fast-track trial.

Decree & Execution

We secure the final decree and file for execution, authorizing attachment and sale of debtor's assets to recover money.
