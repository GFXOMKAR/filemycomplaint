About Consumer Complaints

A consumer complaint is a formal expression of dissatisfaction filed against a seller, manufacturer, or service provider. Under the Consumer Protection Act, 2019, consumers have robust rights to seek legal remedy when goods purchased are defective or services rendered are deficient.

When should you file a consumer complaint? If you have received a faulty product (e.g., electronic appliances, automobiles, mobile phones) and the seller or brand refuses to refund or repair it; or if you have experienced service deficiency (e.g., flight cancellations without refund, banking/insurance delays, real estate project delays, bad hospital treatment); or if you are victimized by misleading advertisements or unfair trade practices.

Common situations include: Non-refund of money by e-commerce platforms, refusal of insurance claims, possession delays by builders, defective cars/gadgets, and hidden charges by telecom or utility companies.

2

Eligibility / Who Can File

Under the Act, a "Consumer" is defined as any person who buys goods or hires/avails services for consideration (payment). This includes:

  • Any individual buying items or availing services for personal use
  • A registered consumer association
  • Central or State Governments
  • Legal heirs or representatives of a deceased consumer

Note: Goods or services obtained for commercial purposes (reselling or commercial business operations) are generally excluded unless they are for earning livelihood by self-employment.

Limitation Period: The complaint must be filed within 2 years from the date on which the cause of action (defect, delay, or dispute) arose.

3

Required Documents

  • Invoice / Bill / Purchase receipt
  • Warranty Card (if applicable)
  • E-mails or chat logs of communication with customer support
  • Photographs or video proof of the defective product
  • Payment transaction screenshots or bank statement
  • Legal Notice sent to the company (if any)
  • Government ID (Aadhaar / Passport / Driving License)
