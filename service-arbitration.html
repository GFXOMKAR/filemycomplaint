About Arbitration & Mediation

Arbitration and Mediation are Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) processes used to resolve civil and commercial conflicts outside of the traditional court system. With court dockets heavily backlogged, ADR offers a faster, private, and specialized forum to settle disputes.

Arbitration: A private adjudication process where the disputing parties appoint a neutral third party (an Arbitrator) to hear evidence and pass a binding decision called an Arbitral Award. Under Section 36 of the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, an arbitral award is enforceable in civil courts exactly like a court decree.

Mediation: A voluntary, non-binding negotiation process where a trained Mediator assists the parties in reaching an amicable settlement. Unlike an arbitrator, a mediator does not pass a judgment but helps the parties draft a mutually agreed Settlement Deed.

2

Eligibility / Who Can File

Parties can initiate arbitration or mediation under the following conditions:

  • A written contract exists between the parties containing an "Arbitration Clause".
  • In the absence of a clause, both parties sign a written agreement to refer their existing dispute to arbitration.
  • Commercial Court disputes where pre-institution mediation is mandatorily ordered by the court.
  • Matrimonial or family disputes referred by a Family Court judge for mediation.
3

Required Documents

  • Agreement / Contract containing the Arbitration clause
  • Written notice invoking arbitration sent to the other party
  • Statement of Claim (with evidence of dispute/default)
  • Correspondence (emails, letters) showing the dispute points
  • Company Registration or Partnership Deed (if commercial)
  • KYC documents of the claimant
4

Step-by-Step Process

Notice Invocation

We draft and send a formal notice under Section 21 invoking the arbitration clause and proposing an arbitrator.

Arbitrator Appointment

If the parties agree, the arbitrator is appointed. If not, we file a petition under Section 11 before the High Court to appoint one.

Filing Claims & Pleadings

The claimant files the Statement of Claim. The respondent files the Statement of Defense and counter-claims (if any).

Tribunal Hearings

The tribunal holds private hearings, examines documents, and hears arguments. Oral witness testimonies are brief.

Passing of Award

The arbitrator passes the final Arbitral Award within the statutory 12-month timeline under Section 29A.

Award Execution

If the losing party does not challenge the award within 90 days, we execute it in civil court to attach assets.
