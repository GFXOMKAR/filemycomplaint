About Civil Litigation

Civil litigation refers to the legal process of resolving disputes between individuals, businesses, or organisations through a court of law — where one party seeks compensation, an injunction, or specific performance from another. Unlike criminal law, civil law deals with private rights and remedies rather than punishment.

When should you file a civil suit? You should consider civil litigation when someone has breached a contract with you, wrongfully occupied your property, caused you financial loss, or failed to fulfil a legally enforceable promise. Civil suits are also appropriate when all other attempts at resolution — negotiation, mediation — have failed.

Common situations include: Recovery of money owed by a person or company; enforcement of a contract that was broken; claiming damages for loss caused by another's negligence; getting a court injunction to stop someone from doing something harmful; disputes over property ownership, tenancy, or succession.

2

Eligibility / Who Can File

Any person, company, or entity that has a legally enforceable right and has suffered a legal injury may file a civil suit. This includes:

  • Indian citizens and residents
  • Registered companies and partnerships
  • Societies, trusts, and institutions
  • Minors and persons of unsound mind (through a legal guardian)

Limitation Period: Most civil suits must be filed within 3 years of the cause of action arising (under the Limitation Act, 1963). Property suits related to possession must be filed within 12 years. It is critical to file promptly to avoid dismissal on grounds of limitation.

Required Documents

  • PAN Card
  • Address Proof (utility bill / rent agreement)
  • Written Agreement / Contract (if any)
  • Correspondence — emails, WhatsApp, letters
  • Bills / Invoices / Payment receipts
  • Bank transaction statements
  • Property documents (if property dispute)
  • Photographs / Videos (evidence)
  • Witness details (name, address, contact)
  • Previous court orders (if any)
  • Legal notice copy (if already sent)

Financial Recovery
