About Property & Land Disputes

Property disputes involve conflicts over real estate assets, including land, apartments, commercial buildings, and boundary spaces. These conflicts arise from invalid title claims, illegal encroachment, breach of sale agreements, tenant refusal to vacate, or builder failure to deliver possession under RERA guidelines.

Encroachment and Illegal Possession: If someone has illegally occupied your land or built a structure over your boundary, you can file a civil suit for possession and injunction under the Specific Relief Act to demolish the illegal structure and restore your ownership.

RERA & Builder Cases: Real estate buyers who do not get flat possession within the agreed timeline can file claims before RERA (Real Estate Regulatory Authority) for a refund of their investment with interest, or demanding immediate completion and compensation.

2

Eligibility / Who Can File

Remedies can be sought by any person or entity with a legal interest in a property, including:

  • Registered property owners (having title deeds or sale deeds)
  • Buyers who have signed a registered agreement to sell
  • Landlords or tenants under a valid lease / tenancy agreement
  • Legal heirs claiming share in ancestral family property

Limitation Period: Suits for declaration of title must be filed within 3 years of the dispute starting. For recovery of possession of immovable property based on title, the limitation period is 12 years.

3

Required Documents

  • Registered Sale Deed / Title Deed / Gift Deed
  • Agreement to Sell / Allotment Letter (from builder)
  • Property Tax receipts / Utility bills
  • Land records (7/12 extract, Index II, or Khata certificate)
  • Approved building plans & maps
  • Rent Agreement (for tenant disputes)
  • Photographs or survey reports of encroachment
  • Correspondence / Legal Notices sent to opposite party

Step-by-Step Process

Title & Document Review

We review your ownership deeds and land records to verify your legal title and rights.

Send Demand / Notice

We issue a formal Legal Notice to the encroacher, tenant, or builder to cease violation or vacate.

Filing in Court / RERA

If notice is ignored, we file a civil suit for possession/injunction or a complaint before RERA.

Interim Injunction Order

We immediately apply for temporary injunction (stay order) to prevent any sale or construction during the case.

Evidence & Local Survey

Court-appointed commissioners or surveyors map the land to verify claims of encroachment.

Decree & Execution

We secure the final decree and execute it with police assistance if necessary to restore possession.
