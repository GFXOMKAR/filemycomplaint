About Cyber Crime & Online Fraud

Cyber crime encompasses any criminal act involving a computer, mobile phone, or the internet. India's Information Technology Act, 2000 and the Indian Penal Code provide strong legal remedies for victims of online fraud, hacking, and digital harassment.

Common types of cyber crime: Online financial fraud (UPI scams, phishing, fake websites), Identity theft (using your Aadhaar/PAN fraudulently), Hacking of social media or bank accounts, Cyber stalking and online harassment, Morphed photos or deepfake abuse, Ransomware and data theft, Fake job/investment fraud.

Critical first step: Report immediately to your bank and file on cybercrime.gov.in. The faster you report, the better the chance of freezing the fraudster's account before the money is withdrawn. Our advocate can help you file the most effective complaint within hours.

2

Eligibility / Who Can File

Any person who has been a victim of cyber crime can file a complaint. There is no court fee for filing on cybercrime.gov.in. Special provisions exist for women and children victims.

  • Victims of online financial fraud (UPI, banking, investment)
  • Victims of identity theft or account hacking
  • Victims of cyber harassment, stalking, or blackmail
  • Women and children victims (dedicated reporting category)
  • Companies and businesses victim of data breach or ransomware

Important: Do NOT share OTPs or personal details with anyone claiming to be from the police or bank. Legitimate authorities will never ask for your OTP.

3

Required Documents

  • Aadhaar Card / Government ID Proof
  • Screenshots of fraudulent messages/emails
  • Bank statement showing unauthorized transaction
  • Transaction ID / UTR number of fraud transfer
  • URLs of fake websites / profiles
  • Call recordings or logs of fraud call
  • OTP/phishing SMS screenshots
  • Email headers of phishing email
  • Complaint already filed with bank (if any)
  • Police complaint copy (if already filed)
  • Account opening documents
  • Any other correspondence with fraudster

Step-by-Step Process

Immediate Report to Bank

Call your bank's 24×7 helpline immediately to block the transaction and flag the fraudster's account.

File on cybercrime.gov.in

Report on the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in). Call the cyber crime helpline: 1930. Our advocate assists with proper filing.

Advocate Reviews Evidence

We review all your screenshots, transactions, and messages to identify the exact IT Act sections and IPC provisions applicable.

FIR with Cyber Crime Cell

A detailed FIR is filed at the local Cyber Crime Cell with all evidence, requesting account freeze and investigation.

Account Freeze & Investigation

Police coordinate with banks and payment gateways to freeze the fraudster's accounts and trace the money trail.

Recovery & Legal Action

Once the fraudster is identified, civil and criminal recovery proceedings are initiated for full refund plus compensation.

Benefits of Taking Legal Action

🔐

Account Freeze

Fraudster's bank account can be frozen within 24 hours to prevent further withdrawal of your money.

💰

Money Recovery

RBI zero-liability policy + cyber investigation can result in full refund of fraudulently transferred amounts.

🚔

Arrest of Fraudster

IT Act offences carry 3–7 years imprisonment, making criminal prosecution a powerful deterrent.

🗑️

Content Removal

Fake profiles and defamatory content can be taken down within 36 hours via court order under IT Act.

🏦

Bank Liability

If the bank failed to protect your account, RBI Ombudsman can hold the bank liable for compensation.

🛡️

Protection from Harassment

Court can issue restraining orders against cyber harassers and stalkers for your ongoing protection.
