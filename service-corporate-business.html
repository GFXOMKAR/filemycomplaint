About Corporate & Business Disputes

Corporate and business disputes encompass legal conflicts arising in commercial settings. These include disagreements between partners in a firm, disputes among company directors or shareholders regarding corporate governance (oppression and mismanagement), breaches of business supply agreements, or insolvency filings under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC).

NCLT & Insolvency (IBC): If a corporate debtor fails to pay outstanding business dues of ₹1 Crore or more, operational or financial creditors can initiate corporate insolvency resolution proceedings (CIRP) before the National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT) to recover debts or restructure the debtor company.

Partnership Disputes: Conflicts in partnership firms are resolved under the Indian Partnership Act, 1932. Common actions involve suits for dissolution of the partnership, rendering of accounts, and distribution of assets.

2

Eligibility / Who Can File

Legal remedies under corporate and business laws can be initiated by:

  • Registered partners of a partnership firm or members of an LLP
  • Shareholders holding at least 10% of issued share capital (for Oppression & Mismanagement suits before NCLT)
  • Creditors (financial or operational) with unpaid commercial dues
  • Directors or promoters seeking to protect their corporate rights or challenge illegal removals
3

Required Documents

  • Certificate of Incorporation (for companies) or Registered Partnership Deed
  • Memorandum and Articles of Association (MOA / AOA)
  • Commercial Contract / Purchase Order / Service Level Agreement (SLA)
  • Invoices, ledger statements, and bank statements showing defaults
  • Shareholder Agreements (SHA) or board resolution copies
  • Correspondence (emails, letters, minutes of meetings) showing the dispute
  • Legal Notices sent under the Companies Act or IBC (Form 3/4 notice)
