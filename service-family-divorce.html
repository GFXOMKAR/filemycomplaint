About Family & Divorce Matters

Family law governs legal relations between family members. This includes marital status changes, property division, protection of children, and maintenance rights. Family courts emphasize amicable settlement but handle complex contested matters with trial proceedings.

Mutual Consent vs. Contested Divorce: A Mutual Consent Divorce is the fastest way to end a marriage when both parties agree on terms like alimony and child custody, taking as little as 6 months. A Contested Divorce is filed when one party wishes to separate based on grounds like cruelty, desertion, adultery, or conversion, and requires formal court trials.

Maintenance & Alimony: Under Section 125 of CrPC and personal laws, a spouse who is unable to maintain themselves can claim monthly maintenance from the other spouse during and after the court case.

2

Eligibility / Who Can File

You can file for family court remedies if you satisfy the following jurisdictional and status criteria:

  • Any legally married spouse seeking divorce, judicial separation, or restitution of conjugal rights.
  • Either spouse seeking child custody or visitation rights.
  • Dependent spouse, children, or parents seeking monthly maintenance.
  • Co-owners of family property seeking partition or share determination.

Jurisdiction: The case can be filed where the marriage was solemnized, where the couple last resided together, or where the wife is currently residing.

3

Required Documents

  • Marriage Certificate or Wedding Invitation Card / Photos
  • Passport-size photographs of husband and wife
  • Address proofs of husband and wife
  • Evidence of grounds for divorce (e.g. medical certificates, chat transcripts, police complaints)
  • Income proof (Salary slip, IT Returns, bank statement) of both parties for maintenance calculation
  • Children's birth certificates (for custody cases)
  • Mutual settlement agreement draft (for mutual consent divorce)

Step-by-Step Process

Consultation & Mediation

We consult with you to check if settlement is possible. If not, we outline the legal options.

Petition Drafting

Our advocates prepare the divorce or maintenance petition, enclosing necessary evidence and assets statements.

Filing in Family Court

The petition is filed before the appropriate Family Court, and summons are issued to the other spouse.

Mandatory Counseling

The family court refers both parties to a court counselor to check if reconciliation or mutual terms can be worked out.

Trial & Evidence

If counseling fails, both parties present evidence and undergo cross-examination regarding maintenance, custody, or allegations.

Final Decree

The judge passes the final order granting divorce, child custody, alimony, and child support payments.
