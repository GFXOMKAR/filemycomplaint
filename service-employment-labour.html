About Employment & Labour Disputes

Employment and labour disputes occur when there is a breach of contract or violation of labour laws by either the employer or the employee. Typical disputes involve wrongful termination without notice, withholding of salary/incentives, failure to pay gratuity or provident fund (PF) benefits, and unsafe working environments.

Wrongful Termination: If an employer terminates you without serving the required notice period mentioned in your employment contract, or without paying your severance package (or full and final settlement), it is a breach of contract. Depending on your designation, you can file a complaint with the Labour Commissioner or file a civil recovery suit.

Workplace Harassment (POSH): The Prevention of Sexual Harassment (POSH) Act protects women from workplace harassment. Companies are legally mandated to have an Internal Committee (IC) to address such complaints, and failures can lead to corporate liability.

Eligibility / Who Can File

This service applies to the following categories of workers and professionals in India:

  • "Workmen" as defined under the Industrial Disputes Act (manual, technical, or clerical workers)
  • Corporate employees and managerial staff (remedies via Civil Courts)
  • Contractual staff and gig workers
  • Women employees experiencing discrimination or harassment

Limitation: Gratuity and wage claims should ideally be filed within 1 year of default, though condonation of delay is possible under specific circumstances.

Required Documents

  • Employment Offer Letter / Appointment Letter / Contract
  • Salary Slips (last 3-6 months)
  • Bank Statement showing salary credits
  • Termination Letter / Resignation Acceptance Email
  • Form 16 or PF Statements
  • Email communications regarding dispute or grievance submission
  • Experience Certificate / Relieving Letter (if issued)

Step-by-Step Process

Consultation & Review

We review your employment contract, termination reason, and wage logs to build a solid legal claim.

Legal Notice to Employer

We draft and send a formal Legal Demand Notice to the HR department and Directors of the company.

Conciliation Proceeding

If they refuse to settle, we file a dispute before the Labour Commissioner for mediation.

Filing in Court / Tribunal

If conciliation fails, the dispute is referred to the Labour Court or a Civil Suit is filed for corporate employees.

Advocate Representation

Our advocates present evidence of wrongful dismissal or salary retention during court hearings.

Final Recovery Order

The court orders the employer to clear dues with interest, pay back wages, or reinstate the employee.
